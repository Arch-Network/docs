title: Multi-party programs powering trading and liquidity
---

Developers and application users have limited access to liquidity in the current Bitcoin capital markets system. They can do peer-to-peer transactions on Discreet-Log-Contracts (DLCs), which limits them to drawing assets from a single account, or an orderbook, which has similar liquidity challenges and only executes when buy/sell prices are matched perfectly. They can also try to use one of the few trading venues that currently exist on the Bitcoin base layer, but these still require holders to give up custody through essentially centralized multisigs to use them.

Arch's structure makes it possible to execute the multi-party contracts necessary for Liquidity Pools (LPs), which are foundational to building the Automated Market Makers (AMMs) that drive trust-minimized trading venues like [Arch Swap](/products/arch-swap). These liquidity pools serve as an instant, permissionless source of liquidity from multiple sources, which borrowers can tap into without relying on DLCs or order books managed off-chain.

## Enabling Bitcoin Capital Markets

### 1. Liquidity Protocols:
Arch makes it possible to develop financial applications that can tap into Bitcoin's massive liquidity pool. This is the foundation for [Arch Swap](/products/arch-swap) — atomic-settlement BTC trading with deep liquidity provided by closed-loop integration with [Arch Lend](/products/arch-lend).

### 2. Lending and Borrowing Markets:
By supporting smart contracts, Arch enables the creation of institutional-grade lending and borrowing markets on Bitcoin. [Arch Lend](/products/arch-lend) lets users lend their Bitcoin or other digital assets in a trustless manner, earning yield, or borrow against their holdings without traditional financial intermediaries — with 300ms liquidation fidelity.

### 3. Yield Strategies:
Arch's infrastructure supports yield strategies and staking mechanisms, offering users new ways to earn returns on their Bitcoin-native asset holdings. [Yield Vaults](/products/yield-vaults) deploy capital across lending markets and structured products to generate yield on Bitcoin.

### 4. Synthetic Assets and Derivatives:
With smart contract capabilities, Arch allows for the creation of synthetic assets and decentralized derivatives markets. Scaled credit on Arch Lend provides the collateral foundation that derivatives, options, futures, and structured products need to scale.

### 5. Cross-Chain Liquidity:
Arch's focus on interoperability — supported by partners like Bitfrost — facilitates seamless interaction between Bitcoin and other blockchains, expanding the range of possibilities for financial applications.

By enabling these use cases, Arch Network builds the financial infrastructure layer for Bitcoin capital markets — turning Bitcoin from a store of value into the foundation for global capital markets.
